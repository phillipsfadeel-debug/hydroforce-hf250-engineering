# hydroforce-hf250-engineering
Industrial Engineering Project: Complete product industrialization, process routing optimization, and automated system integration for the HydroForce HF-250 PRO. Driven by Lean Manufacturing principles, work study metrics, and systematic facility layout design.  
HydroForce HF-250 PRO: Industrial Systems Design & Facility Layout📌 Project OverviewThis repository contains the end-to-end product industrialization, commercial feasibility model, process workflow architecture, and plant layout design for HydroForce Industries (Pty) Ltd, based in Epping Industrial, Cape Town. The primary objective of this project is to launch the HydroForce HF-250 PRO—a heavy-duty, locally manufactured 250 Bar electric pressure washer designed as an agile, cost-effective alternative to imported European brands. 🚀 Engineering Execution Phases📈 Phase 1: Product Industrialization & Financial FeasibilityAccomplished in accordance with FAM360S Project Submission 1 Assessment Rubric 2025.pdf.  Market Validation: Targets a 0.8% market share (ZAR 6.64 million annual revenue) by Year 5 within the ZAR 1.2 billion South African industrial cleaning sector.  Financial Modeling: Accounts for a total initial capital setup investment of R2,500,000 with an annual fixed operating cost of R1,440,000.  Breakeven Metrics: Calculated mathematical breakeven at 185 units/year with an estimated payback period of 3.6 years and a 5-year ROI of 218%.  Sourcing Architecture: Features a structured Bill of Materials (BOM) detailing 14 key components (e.g., AISI 4130 base plates, 316 annealed stainless steel frames) mapped directly to a strategic Make-or-Buy decision model. ⚙️ Phase 2: Process Engineering & Macro-Flow SynthesisAccomplished in accordance with FAM360S Project Submission 2 Assessment Rubric 2025.pdf.  Operations Mapping: Synthesis of production style selection backed by an integrated Assembly Chart and Operation Process Chart (OPC) to map the sequential assembly of the high-pressure pump and 7.5 kW AC motor.  Method Engineering: Compilation of comprehensive Flow Process Charts (FPC) tracking material delays, inspections, transport distances, and storage intervals.  Multi-Criteria Optimization: Developed and analyzed two distinct Master Flow Patterns using weighted evaluation matrices to minimize material handling footprint, logistics intersection hazards, and backtracking.  📐 Phase 3: Facility Layout Construction & Work DesignAccomplished in accordance with FAM360S: FINAL PROJECT SUBMISSION: ASSESSMENT SHEET.  Systematic Layout Planning (SLP): Modeled relationship proximity across core plant departments using Activity Relationship Charts (ARC) and Worksheets to minimize plant logistics friction.  Ergonomics & Work Study: Designed human-centric standalone workstations engineered via Modapts Standards (Modular Arrangement of Predetermined Time Standards) to ensure high utilization and worker safety.  Assembly Line Balancing: Balanced manufacturing processing speeds to match localized demand cycle targets, reducing workstation idle time and eliminating work-in-progress (WIP) accumulation.  Dimensioned Footprint Allocation: Formulated strict dimensional parameters (length, width, area $m^2$) for core logistic zones (Receiving, Shipping, Warehousing) and secondary infrastructure (Toolrooms, Cafeteria, Ablutions).
